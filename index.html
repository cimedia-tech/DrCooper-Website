Dr. Tony Lee Cooper, Sr. is an accomplished educator and researcher based in Statesville, North Carolina. With an Ed.D. focused on academic success factors among first-generation African-American students at Historically Black Colleges, he brings deep expertise in student retention and resilience-based interventions.

His dissertation research identified personal resilience as a statistically significant predictor of GPA (p = .012), supporting evidence-based approaches to improve student retention and academic outcomes.
